PROOF Analysis: Why ALUGON® He50 for Thick Aluminum

P - Problem: Thick Aluminum Welding Challenges

  • Insufficient penetration: Pure argon lacks heat input for 6mm+ sections
  • Multi-pass requirements: Increases distortion, time, and defect risk
  • Heat-affected zone (HAZ): Excessive width reduces mechanical properties
  • Lack of fusion: Cold lap defects at root and sidewall interfaces
  • Productivity bottlenecks: Slow travel speeds on thick sections
  • Porosity in thick sections: Hydrogen entrapment in deep weld pools

R - Research: Standards and Real Compositions

  • ISO 14175: I2-ArHe-50 classification for thick aluminum (6-25mm)
  • AWS D1.2: Recommended for structural aluminum >6mm thickness
  • EN 1011-4: Specifies minimum 50% He for aluminum >8mm
  • DIN 32526: German standard for thick aluminum welding gases
  • Composition tolerance: ±1% He ensures consistent heat input
  • Purity specification: 99.996% minimum for aerospace applications
  • Moisture content: <2ppm H₂O prevents hydrogen porosity

Limitations & Considerations

Not Recommended For

  • Thin aluminum sheets (<6mm) - use ALUGON standard instead
  • TIG welding applications - use pure argon
  • Pulse welding on thin sections
  • Applications requiring minimal heat input
  • Budget-sensitive projects where multi-pass is acceptable

Special Considerations

  • Higher gas cost (~60% premium over pure argon)
  • Requires proper gas flow control - helium rises rapidly
  • May require different wire feed speeds due to increased heat
  • Best used with aluminum-specific MIG equipment
  • Outdoor welding requires wind protection due to light helium
