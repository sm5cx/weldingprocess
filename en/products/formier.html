Usage Guidelines

Purging Setup

Pipe Diameter Flow Rate Purge Time Monitoring
25-50mm 5-8 L/min 10-15 min O₂ meter required
50-100mm 8-15 L/min 15-25 min O₂ < 50 ppm target
100-200mm 15-25 L/min 25-40 min O₂ < 20 ppm for critical work
200mm+ 25-40 L/min 40-60 min Continuous monitoring required

Setup Instructions

  1. Install purge dams or inflatable plugs to contain atmosphere
  2. Connect FORMIER supply to inlet end with appropriate flow control
  3. Install oxygen monitoring probe at exit end
  4. Begin purging at 125% of calculated flow rate
  5. Monitor oxygen levels until target purity is achieved
  6. Maintain flow during welding and cooling cycle
  7. Continue purge until weld temperature drops below 400°C

Safety Considerations

Critical Safety Requirements

  • FORMIER contains hydrogen - ensure adequate ventilation
  • Monitor for explosive atmosphere accumulation (>4% H₂)
  • Use hydrogen-rated equipment and connections
  • Implement hot work permits for hydrogen-containing gases
  • Train personnel on hydrogen safety procedures
  • Maintain oxygen monitoring throughout operation

Optimization Tips

  • Use soluble purge dams to minimize contamination risk
  • Calculate purge volume as 5x internal volume for complete exchange
  • Position oxygen probe at highest point where air accumulates
  • Maintain slight positive pressure to prevent air ingress
  • Consider FORMIER 10 for enhanced reduction in critical applications
  • Use flow restrictors to maintain consistent pressure

Comparison with Alternatives

FORMIER® vs Pure Nitrogen

Pure Nitrogen

When to use pure nitrogen: Titanium welding, reactive metal applications, or when hydrogen content must be zero.

FORMIER advantage: Active reduction capability removes existing oxides, better root penetration, and superior root quality on stainless steels. The hydrogen component provides cleaning action that pure nitrogen cannot achieve.
