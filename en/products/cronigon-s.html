CRONIGON® S (98% Ar + 2% N₂) is the specialist gas for duplex stainless steel welding [S1]. The controlled nitrogen addition maintains the critical 40-60% austenite-ferrite balance while preventing sigma phase formation during cooling (600-1000°C range) [S2]. Superior to pure argon for MAG welding but limited to duplex grades only - not suitable for austenitic 304/316 steels due to nitrogen embrittlement risk [S3].

Usage Guidelines

Recommended Parameters

Critical parameter control: gas flow increases with thickness (12-25 L/min range), while heat input must stay ≤3.0 kJ/mm maximum to prevent detrimental sigma phase formation during cooling [S2, S3].

Material Thickness Gas Flow Rate Heat Input Limit Special Notes
3-6mm 12-15 L/min ≤1.5 kJ/mm Single pass, controlled cooling
6-12mm 15-18 L/min ≤2.0 kJ/mm Monitor interpass temp ≤150°C
12-20mm 18-22 L/min ≤2.5 kJ/mm Multi-pass, strict heat input control
20mm+ 20-25 L/min ≤3.0 kJ/mm Consider TIG root pass

Setup Instructions

  1. Use high-purity regulator designed for nitrogen-containing mixtures
  2. Set flow rate to 15 L/min initially, adjust based on shielding requirements
  3. Select duplex-specific filler metal (ER2209, ER2594, etc.)
  4. Set welding parameters for controlled heat input (monitor kJ/mm)
  5. Maintain consistent push angle 10-15° for optimal gas coverage
  6. Use stainless steel cleaning procedures - no carbon contamination
  7. Pre-flow 2-3 seconds, post-flow 5-7 seconds minimum

Optimization Tips

  • Strict interpass temperature control - maximum 150°C to prevent sigma formation
  • Use ferrite measuring instruments to verify phase balance (target 40-60% austenite)
  • Monitor heat input carefully - duplex sensitive to excessive thermal cycles
  • Consider backing gas for critical applications (matching CRONIGON S composition)
  • Rapid cooling beneficial - avoid slow cooling through 600-1000°C range
  • Maintain nitrogen cylinder upright to prevent gas separation

Limitations & Considerations

Not Recommended For

  • Austenitic stainless steels (304, 316 grades)
  • Ferritic stainless steels (409, 430 grades)
  • Carbon steel applications (nitrogen embrittlement risk)
  • Applications requiring maximum penetration (low CO2 content)
  • Thin sections where heat input cannot be controlled
  • Repairs where parent metal phase balance unknown

Special Considerations

  • Heat input control critical - exceeding limits causes phase imbalance
  • Requires duplex-specific filler metals with appropriate composition
  • Post-weld phase balance verification recommended for critical applications
  • More expensive than standard stainless gases due to nitrogen content
  • Storage considerations - nitrogen can separate if cylinder not upright
  • Requires operator training in duplex welding principles

Frequently Asked Questions

Can CRONIGON® S be used for austenitic stainless steels like 304 or 316?

No. CRONIGON® S is specifically formulated for duplex stainless steels only. The 2% nitrogen content can cause embrittlement in austenitic grades like 304/316. Use CRONIGON® 2 (Ar+2%CO₂) for austenitic applications [S3].

What's the difference between CRONIGON® S and pure argon for duplex welding?

Pure argon lacks nitrogen, leading to ferrite-rich weld metal that compromises duplex properties. CRONIGON® S adds precisely 2% nitrogen to maintain the optimal 40-60% austenite balance critical for duplex strength and corrosion resistance [S2].

Why is heat input control more critical with duplex steels?

Excessive heat input (>3.0 kJ/mm) causes sigma phase formation in the 600-1000°C temperature range during cooling, severely embrittling the weld. CRONIGON® S nitrogen helps suppress sigma formation but cannot overcome excessive thermal input [S2].

Is CRONIGON® S more expensive than standard welding gases?

Yes, typically 40-60% higher cost than pure argon due to specialized nitrogen blending and quality control requirements. However, this prevents costly rework from phase imbalance and eliminates post-weld heat treatment needs [S4].

Can I use CRONIGON® S for TIG welding of duplex steels?

Yes, though TIG welding allows more precise heat control where pure argon might suffice for root passes. CRONIGON® S is essential for MAG/MIG welding where faster cooling rates require nitrogen to maintain phase balance [S1].
