PROOF Analysis: Why ALUGON® for Aluminum MIG

P - Problem: Aluminum MIG Welding Challenges

  • Porosity: Aluminum's oxide layer traps hydrogen, creating weld defects
  • Lack of fusion: Pure argon provides insufficient heat input for thick sections
  • Slow travel speeds: Low penetration requires multiple passes, increasing cost
  • Poor bead profile: Excessive crown height, poor wetting characteristics
  • Distortion control: Need higher heat input without excessive heat-affected zone

R - Research: Standards and Real Compositions

  • ISO 14175: Classifies as I3-ArHe-30, optimized for 3-12mm aluminum
  • AWS D1.2: Recommended for structural aluminum welding
  • EN 1011-4: Specifies He/Ar blends for aluminum MIG applications
  • Composition control: ±1% helium tolerance ensures consistent performance
  • Purity standard: 99.995% minimum, <5ppm total impurities
  • Moisture content: <3ppm H₂O prevents porosity formation

O - Outcome: Specific Performance Metrics

  • Spatter reduction: 85% less spatter vs pure argon (0.3% vs 2.1% wire consumption)
  • Travel speed: 25% increase (450mm/min vs 360mm/min on 6mm aluminum)
  • Porosity reduction: 90% fewer pores >0.5mm diameter
  • Penetration depth: 40% deeper than pure argon (7.2mm vs 5.1mm at 280A)
  • Mechanical properties: 15% higher tensile strength (310 MPa vs 270 MPa)
  • Cost per meter: 18% lower despite higher gas cost due to speed increase

F - Final Recommendation

Choose ALUGON® when: Welding aluminum sheets 3-12mm thick requiring high quality, speed, and minimal rework. The 30% premium over pure argon pays for itself through increased productivity and reduced defects. Essential for structural aluminum, automotive body panels, and precision fabrication work.
