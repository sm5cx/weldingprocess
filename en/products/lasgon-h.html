PROOF Analysis: Why LASGON® H for High-Speed Cutting

P - Problem: Thick Section Cutting Limitations

  • Speed bottlenecks: Nitrogen cutting too slow for high-volume production
  • Heat dissipation: Poor heat removal from thick sections causes quality issues
  • Productivity pressure: Need maximum throughput on expensive laser equipment
  • Thick stainless challenges: Poor edge quality above 8mm thickness
  • Economic competitiveness: Laser cutting losing to plasma/waterjet on speed
  • Beam absorption: Insufficient energy coupling in reflective materials

R - Research: Hydrogen Cutting Physics

  • Thermal conductivity: 7x higher than N₂ enables rapid heat removal
  • Beam absorption: Enhanced plasma formation increases energy coupling
  • Molecular properties: Smallest molecule provides superior kerf cleaning
  • ISO 11554: Hydrogen purity specifications for laser applications
  • Safety standards: EN 60825 compliance for hydrogen laser systems
  • Cutting mechanism: Pure thermal cutting without oxidation
  • Flow dynamics: Low density requires pressure optimization

Safety Considerations

⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

  • Hydrogen safety training: All operators must complete certified H₂ training
  • Gas detection systems: Mandatory H₂ leak detection with auto-shutoff
  • Ventilation requirements: Enhanced ventilation to prevent accumulation
  • Electrical classification: Class I, Division 2 electrical systems required
  • Hot work permits: Special procedures for maintenance and setup
  • Emergency procedures: Documented response plans for leak incidents
