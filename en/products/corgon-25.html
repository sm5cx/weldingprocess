CORGON® 25 (75% Ar + 25% CO₂) is the premium choice for heavy structural steel welding [S1]. Delivers maximum penetration depth (up to 40% deeper than 18% CO₂ mixes) and faster deposition rates ideal for thick plate applications over 15mm [S2]. However, higher spatter levels than lower CO₂ variants - not recommended for thin materials below 6mm due to excessive heat input [S3].

Technical Specifications

CORGON® 25 maximizes penetration power with 25% CO₂ content (higher than standard 18%) while maintaining 75% argon for arc stability, meeting both ISO 14175 (M21-ArC-25) and AWS A5.32 (C-25) standards with enhanced thermal conductivity for thick plate applications [S1, S2].

Usage Guidelines

Recommended Parameters

Material Thickness Gas Flow Rate Nozzle Distance Special Notes
15-25mm 18-22 L/min 15-20mm Spray transfer recommended >280A
25-40mm 20-25 L/min 18-25mm Multi-pass with controlled interpass temp
40-60mm 22-28 L/min 20-30mm Deep groove prep, backing gas helpful
60mm+ 25-30 L/min 25-35mm Narrow gap welding preferred

Setup Instructions

  1. Use heavy-duty regulator rated for continuous high-flow operation
  2. Set initial flow rate to 20 L/min, adjust based on penetration needs
  3. Ensure wire feed speed matches increased deposition demands (6-12 m/min)
  4. Set voltage 2-3V higher than standard Ar/CO2 for same wire feed speed
  5. Maintain consistent 15-20° push angle for maximum penetration
  6. Use larger contact tip size to handle increased current density
  7. Pre-flow 2-3 seconds to establish stable gas envelope

Optimization Tips

  • Monitor interpass temperature closely - maximum 150°C for structural grades
  • Use weaving technique on thick root passes to ensure complete fusion
  • Increase travel speed if excessive penetration causes burn-through
  • Consider backing gas for full penetration butt joints over 25mm
  • Use pulse welding mode to control heat input on critical applications
  • Employ wind shields in outdoor applications - high CO2 sensitive to drafts

Limitations & Considerations

Not Recommended For

  • Thin materials under 6mm (excessive penetration risk)
  • Stainless steel welding (carbon pickup from CO2)
  • Aluminum welding (incompatible with process)
  • Root passes requiring precise penetration control
  • Applications requiring ultra-clean bead appearance
  • Short-circuit transfer mode (unstable arc characteristics)

Special Considerations

  • Higher spatter levels than CORGON 18 - requires more post-weld cleanup
  • Aggressive arc may cause undercut if travel speed too low
  • CO2 dissociation increases weld pool temperature - monitor HAZ
  • Higher current requirements - ensure adequate power source capacity
  • More sensitive to surface contamination than lower CO2 mixes
  • Requires experienced operators for positional welding

Comparison with Alternatives

CORGON 25 vs CORGON 18

CORGON 18

When to use CORGON 18: General structural work, thinner sections (3-15mm), applications requiring lower spatter, or when bead appearance is critical.

CORGON 25 advantage: Superior penetration (35-40% deeper), higher deposition rates (15-25% faster), better sidewall fusion in thick sections, and fewer passes required for heavy plate welding.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I choose CORGON® 25 over CORGON® 18?

Choose CORGON® 25 for thick plate applications over 15mm where maximum penetration is critical. The extra 7% CO₂ increases penetration by up to 40% but produces more spatter. Use CORGON® 18 for thinner materials and cleaner weld appearance [S2].

Is CORGON® 25 suitable for thin sheet metal welding?

No. CORGON® 25 is optimized for thick sections (15mm+). For thin materials below 6mm, the high heat input causes excessive distortion and burn-through. Use CORGON® 10 instead [S3].

Can CORGON® 25 be used with flux-cored wires?

Yes, CORGON® 25 is excellent with flux-cored wires for heavy structural applications. The high CO₂ content complements the flux system for deep penetration and high deposition rates in thick plate fabrication [S1].

Why does CORGON® 25 produce more spatter than lower CO₂ mixes?

Higher CO₂ content creates more aggressive arc characteristics and thermal energy. While this increases penetration, it also generates more spatter. The trade-off is worthwhile for thick plate where penetration is the priority [S2].

What's the maximum thickness suitable for CORGON® 25?

CORGON® 25 effectively penetrates up to 75mm with proper multi-pass technique. Above this thickness, consider preheating or switching to submerged arc welding processes for optimal productivity and quality [S3].
