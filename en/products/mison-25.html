PROOF Analysis: Why MISON® 25 for Heavy Structural

P - Problem: Heavy Section Welding Challenges

  • Lack of fusion: Insufficient penetration in thick structural sections
  • Multi-pass requirements: Increases distortion, time, and defect probability
  • Heat-affected zone: Wide HAZ reduces structural integrity
  • Impact toughness: Critical for seismic and dynamic loading applications
  • Productivity pressure: Need single-pass capability on thick sections
  • Code compliance: AWS D1.1/D1.8 requirements for critical structures

R - Research: High-CO₂ Tri-Mix Science

  • Penetration mechanics: 25% CO₂ provides optimal heat input for thick sections
  • Micro-oxygen effect: 0.15-0.4% O₂ optimizes metal transfer at high currents
  • AWS D1.1 testing: Qualified for seismic structural applications
  • Impact toughness data: Superior performance at -40°C compared to binary mixes
  • Metallurgical benefits: Refined microstructure from controlled oxidation
  • Fatigue performance: Enhanced crack growth resistance in cyclic loading
  • Single-pass capability: Full penetration up to 20mm thickness

Welding Position Guidelines

  • Flat (1G): Maximum penetration, optimal for single-pass thick sections
  • Horizontal (2G): Excellent for fillet welds, manage higher heat input
  • Vertical (3G): Reduce current 15-20%, use uphill progression
  • Overhead (4G): Not recommended for thick sections, use MISON 18

Advantages & Performance

Structural Benefits

  • Superior impact toughness for seismic and dynamic loading
  • Reduced susceptibility to brittle fracture
  • Enhanced fatigue resistance for cyclic loading
  • Consistent mechanical properties across weld cross-section
  • Reduced sensitivity to cooling rate variations

Production Benefits

  • Single-pass capability reduces welding time by 40%
  • Lower distortion due to concentrated heat input
  • Reduced grinding and preparation between passes
  • Enhanced productivity on thick structural connections
  • Fewer quality control rejections

Limitations & Considerations

Not Recommended For

  • Thin sections (<12mm) where heat input must be controlled
  • Overhead welding positions on thick sections
  • Applications requiring minimal spatter (use MISON 18)
  • Stainless steel or non-ferrous materials
  • Root passes in pressure vessel applications

Special Considerations

  • Higher heat input requires distortion control measures
  • May require preheat on high-carbon or thick sections
  • Enhanced spatter compared to 18% CO₂ mixes
  • Requires experienced operators for positional welding
  • Post-weld stress relief may be required on thick sections
